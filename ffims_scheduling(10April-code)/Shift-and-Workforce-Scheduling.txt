Based on the system requirements and database design for the FFIMS project, the Shift and Workforce Scheduling module can be divided into the following four submodules:

### 1. Rostering and Shift Management
This submodule focuses on the core structural planning of the workforce. It manages the **weekly 5-in/5-out rotation** system, where half the team works while the other half rests. 
*   **Key Functions:** Assigning specific shift types such as **Bus Assigned** (regular routes) versus **Standby Duty** (ad-hoc requests). 
*   **Special Features:** It includes a **University Events Integration** component for bulk shift creation during peak periods like graduation or conference weeks.
*   **Interface:** A **Drag-and-Drop Scheduling** board for supervisors with real-time conflict detection to prevent overlapping assignments.

### 2. Leave Management and Real-Time Availability
This submodule tracks the current and future presence of workers to ensure operational coverage.
*   **Leave Workflow:** A digital system for employees to submit leave requests and for supervisors to approve or reject them via centralized cards. It also tracks **accrued and used leave balances**.
*   **Availability Board:** A real-time status board that displays whether a driver is **On Duty, Standby, On Leave, or Unavailable**.
*   **Mobile Connectivity:** Features a "one-tap" quick status update screen for drivers to reduce manual communication like phone calls.

### 3. Overtime and Labor Compliance
This submodule ensures that the distribution of extra work is both fair and legal.
*   **Fairness Metrics:** It uses algorithms to distribute overtime based on a hierarchy of **seniority, overtime history, and skill matching** to maintain an equitable workload.
*   **Compliance Engine:** The system monitors adherence to the **Zimbabwe Labor Act**, specifically tracking maximum weekly hours and mandatory rest periods to prevent driver fatigue.
*   **Financial Tracking:** It logs overtime reasons and applicable rates (e.g., 1.5x regular pay) to assist in cost projections and payroll accuracy.

### 4. Skill Profiling and Intelligent Matching
This submodule aligns employee capabilities with specific operational needs, particularly for the maintenance **zoning system**.
*   **Employee Profiles:** Maintains detailed records of **license classes, vehicle experience, and certifications** (e.g., first aid).
*   **Matching Algorithm:** Uses AI-powered recommendations to suggest the best worker for a task based on their proficiency in areas like **brush cutting, garden work, or maintenance**.
*   **Gap Analysis:** Identifies training needs and career development paths by comparing current workforce capabilities against organizational requirements.
